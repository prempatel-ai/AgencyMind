SEO Services — Standard Operating Procedures & Checklists

================================================================================
1. ON-PAGE SEO AUDIT STEPS
================================================================================

Step 1 — Crawl Analysis: Run the client's website through Screaming Frog SEO Spider or Sitebulb. Identify broken links (404s), redirect chains, missing meta titles/descriptions, duplicate content, and orphan pages.

Step 2 — Content Audit: Review the top 20 pages by organic traffic. Check for thin content (under 300 words), keyword cannibalization, and outdated information. Flag pages that need rewriting or consolidation.

Step 3 — Technical Audit: Check core web vitals (LCP under 2.5s, FID under 100ms, CLS under 0.1), mobile friendliness via Google's Mobile-Friendly Test, and indexation status via Google Search Console. Note any robots.txt or sitemap issues.

Step 4 — Structured Data: Verify that appropriate schema markup is in place — Article schema for blog posts, LocalBusiness schema for location pages, FAQ schema for knowledge base pages, and Product schema for e-commerce pages.

Step 5 — Competitor Comparison: Compare the client's top 5 landing pages against the top 3 competitors for the same keywords. Note gaps in content depth, internal linking structure, and backlink profiles.

================================================================================
2. KEYWORD RESEARCH PROCESS
================================================================================

Step 1 — Seed List: Gather 20-30 seed keywords from the client based on their services, products, and target audience questions. Use Ahrefs or Google Keyword Planner for volume data.

Step 2 — Cluster Grouping: Group keywords into topical clusters of 5-10 related terms. Each cluster will inform one cornerstone content piece supported by 3-4 cluster articles. Target keywords with 100-1,000 monthly search volume and medium competition.

Step 3 — Search Intent Mapping: For each keyword cluster, label the intent as informational (blog post), navigational (landing page), commercial (comparison page), or transactional (product page). Create a content brief for each target keyword.

Step 4 — SERP Analysis: Review the current top 10 results for each target keyword. Note the featured snippets, People Also Ask boxes, and image packs. Structure content to compete for featured snippet positioning.

Step 5 — Tracking Setup: Add all target keywords to the client's Google Search Console and a rank tracking tool (AccuRanker or SEMrush). Set up weekly rank tracking and monthly reporting.

================================================================================
3. META TAG OPTIMIZATION GUIDE
================================================================================

Title Tag Formula: Primary Keyword — Secondary Keyword | Brand Name (55-60 characters). Each page must have a unique meta title. Avoid keyword stuffing — use the primary keyword once naturally at the start.

Meta Description Formula: Include primary keyword, a value proposition, and a call to action (150-160 characters). Write for click-through rate, not just keyword matching. Example: "Learn how our WordPress maintenance service keeps your site secure, fast, and up-to-date. Get a free audit today."

Open Graph Tags: Every page must have og:title (60 chars), og:description (160 chars), and og:image (1200x630px at 72dpi). Use Yoast SEO or Rank Math to auto-generate from the featured image.

================================================================================
4. MONTHLY REPORTING STRUCTURE
================================================================================

Executive Summary (1 page): Overall organic traffic trend, top-performing pages, major wins, and areas needing attention.

Performance Metrics: Organic sessions (month-over-month and year-over-year), keyword rankings movement (improved, declined, new), conversion rate from organic traffic, and average position in SERPs.

Technical Health: Core web vitals status, indexation count, crawl errors found and fixed, and page speed scores.

Content Performance: Top 5 pages by organic traffic, bottom 5 pages by organic traffic, new content published this month, and content update recommendations.

Competitor Watch: Top 3 competitor movements — new content they published, new backlinks acquired, and ranking changes.

Action Plan for Next Month: 3-5 specific action items with assigned owner and deadline.
